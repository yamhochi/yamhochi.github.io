  • What is an access score?
    • Access Scores

      Reducing travel distances to a diversity of jobs, open space, facilities and services (including retail and health services), and reducing automobile dependency, can have a range of environmental, social and economic benefits that can improve quality of life. It can promote equity of access and opportunity, have health benefits as a result of higher active transport participation, and can moreover result in a reduction of greenhouse gas emissions and pollution. With shorter travel times the productivity of an area can increase, along with diversity, vitality, innovation and creativity, and have positive impact on the attractiveness of an urban area as a whole.

      Train Stations
        Public transport: not only does improving the accessibility and connectivity of the public transport network ensure higher mobility and opportunity for all people, especially those without access to a car, but it ensures that a population’s needs are met while reducing emissions and traffic congestion.
    • 1 minutes from
      Shopping Centres
        Retail: Often forming the core of a strategic centre, ensuring appropriate access (preferably within walking distance) to retail can create vibrant activity centres and meeting points, while providing the ability to acquire necessary goods and services.
    • 1 minutes from
      Schools
        Education (primary schools): Access to education can attract families and promote a diversity of demographics in an urban areas.
    • 1 minutes from
      Hospitals
        Hospitals (health): With access to healthcare considered a right in Australia, proximity to healthcare services can promote liveability and wellbeing.
