Sharing and Enabling Environmental Data

August 2016

Sketching Wireframing User Journey Mapping Information Architecture Peer review

The platform was being developed as a resource for everyone in the community, including landowners, researchers, local government, industry and special interest groups. There was a strong focus on visualization, discoverability in order to help the community find what they needed.

The challenge

"Map Search" was an important feature of the site, users requested an interface that imitated Google's place search workflow. But due to platform constraints, the final product was not as intuitive as anticipated.

I designed a solution to work around the technical constraints. I also persuaded the business to invest more resources on this storey due to its complexity and overarching importance to the platform.

Solutions

User Journey Mapping, Wireframing and Prototyping

Users were asked to provide feedback on the prototype and draw out what was expected from the map search interface. User journey maps, sketches and wireframes were used throughout the process to help my team better understand users' mental model and success paths for this functionality.

Information Architecture

Bottlenecks were identified in the Information Architecture of the site. I implemented a solution that streamlined and consolidated the overall Index for Search. This gave the team more flexibility to query and construct results and present them in a way that made sense to the users.

Peer review and validation

Working in pairs, we made sure that decisions were sense-checked and validated before transforming them into code. This built stronger alliances between teams and we were able to deliver an improved solution ahead of schedule.

Results

Users found the new interface helped achieve their goals much faster. This dramatically improved the number of queries performed, lowered the number of abandoned sessions, and the testing team gave its stamp of approval. The site is currently being reviewed by the working commitee and will go live in mid September.
