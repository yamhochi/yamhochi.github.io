What I'm working on...

A Data Portal for the Environment

According to the NSW Gas Plan,

" The Government intends to play its part by reforming legislative and regulatory requirements and using an open access approach to information. We are currently scoping a whole-of-government environmental data portal, which will become the centrepiece of a new and open approach to data on gas activities. A community website will also be established to allow the community to interact with information on all titles and applications for gas and other resources titles through a state wide map."

I've been lucky enough to be part of a small team building this exact portal. The product is currently in Alpha Stage and here's a sneak peek at what my team has built so far.

I have to say, all those times working as an analyst at SGS came in extremely handy as I get to share with my team how I, as a user of environmental data, would like to use this data portal. Of course I say this some degree of exaggeration as there are other personas that I have to take care of (like the mum and pop users who are just curious. and not necessarily sure what they are looking for in this rocketship of a portal).

Then, there's also making sure that what I've mocked up can actually be built by the developers (including myself) who are taking care of the various CMSs, databases, Mapping platforms, Search Engines that will make these things talk to each other seamlessly and effortlessly.

I believe stage 2 of this project will be even more exciting as the data visualisation and branding specialists will be brought in to make it look good and do cool things. But for now I gotta make sure our product is minimally viable, functional and usable.
