Simplified Information for Mobility and Orientation

June 2014

Interviewing Usability testing Benchmarking Accessibility

The University of New South Wales and Ramsey Stewart Industrial Design (RSID) have secured a four-year Australian Research Council linkage project to explore the development of a Mobility and Location Information System for the Blind and Visually Impaired (BVI).

The challenge

The UNSW geospatial lab has produced an indoor mapping technology to assist blind and visually impaired users orientate themselves in complex environments. This project addresses the issues concerned with the design challenges faced by visually impaired users.

Solutions

User research

Literature review and interviews were conducted during this stage to gather information about user behaviour and mental model of the users for similar applications.

Wireframes and mockups

Low fidelity mockups were produced to depict the proposed solution. Because users could not physically view the product, extra efforts were put in to ensure wireframes were screen-readable and were able to demonstrate the application logic.

Usability testing and benchmarking

Design and functionality were validated through further testing and heuristic evaluations. Usability metrics were derived and were used to determine the success of each design component.

Results

Success path was achieved in shorter time compared to similar products in the market, with an overall success rate of 80%.

Improved user satisfaction and confidence in comparison with the alpha prototype.

The design framework and recommendations were published in a peer-reviewed journal, creating awareness for mobile accessibility and best practices.
