SEED online mapping portal

Client: NSW Department of Finance and Innovation
/ prototyping / front-end development

SEED (Sharing and Enabling Environmental Data) is a plaform built for storing, sharing and visualising environmental information. It empowers the community to search for and analyse large amounts of data when making critical decisions about the environment.

Map search interaction

People were asked to provide feedback on the prototype and draw out what was expected from the map search interface. Having a mid fidelity wireframe helped gathered everyone's thoughts more clearly helped us better align expectations.

Breaking down each component

Trying out different ways give users more flexibility to search and filter.

Breaking down different states of interaction.

Pitching the team some ideas how we could make search much easier with preview option.

  • Spatial query

    There were a few challenges in terms of information architecture and how spatial queries work in general. Users wanted an intuitive search, browse and visualise experience especially when interacting with datasets with geolocation attributes.

    expand_more

    But in reality, data were sporadic across the platform and had various performance issues. In order to set a strong baseline, I mapped out a centralised lookup table that was then used as the single source of truth for querying the right data.

    Another challenge is regarding how spatial search usually returns the whole layer based on where the centroid sits. Rather than relying on this method, I proposed a preview functionality to help viewers decide whether the dataset concerns their area of interest. This significantly reduced unecessary loading from the database and allow users perform a more targeted search flow more effectively.
