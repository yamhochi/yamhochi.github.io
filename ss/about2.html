September 2016

5 minute read

I took a detour from my design career. That turned out to be the best decision I've ever made. Here are some lessons I learnt from those mistakes.

Whe I first started as a designer, I worked long hours, wasn't paid really well. Nobody seemed to care too much about what I did as long as I did what I was told, and delivered on time. Eventually my interest ran out and became

2009-2011

The first 1-2 years of graphic design journey was rather underwhelming. I was naive to think that the creative ’freedom’ at school can easily be replicated in the real world. I questioned a lot about the design sacrifices I had to make because of business decisions. Finally, I gave up and moved back to Australia.

2013-2015

Four years ago I landed a consulting job in Sydney, a career I never saw myself getting into. This job turned out to be the best thing that happened to me. It taught me 2 things that were important to my professional beliefs.

Persuasion

I am a firm believer of ‘actions speak louder than words’. But someone once told me, “Actions are nothing without words” - Without one or the other, neither of them matter. That’s when I started speaking up more in order to build up confidence and reputation. When people started noticing me, they started noticing my work as well.

Writing

People see a well educated person when they see a well written article. The same logic applies to the opposite end of the spectrum. As an art student, writing was never my strong suit. Being in consulting, what you write is who you are, so, being able to write well matters a lot.

Holding these 2 lessons close to my heart, I found myself back in the design industry, but this time in software.

2016

I was still a rookie when I started, but my willingness to learn and adapt helped me settle in the new and fast changing environment. My persuasiveness, communication and awareness around clients reflected my professionalism and understanding of the business. Combined with my now more versatile coding and prototyping skills, I got into places where I can have a voice and can make real impact on project.

Looking back, I finally figured out why I had to make those design sacrifices in those first 2 years. I feel like I've achieved more in 5 months than I ever could in those first 2 years.

The project was a success and we all moved on with some valueble lessons.

It's no secret that I'm now looking for a job and I am sticking with my childhood dream - being a good designer.

I may not have the most traditional design career or a high profile portfolio, but what I have to offer is dedication, enthusiasm and a never stop learning attitude.

I have never turned down the opportunity to try something new because I know I can always learn from my mistakes. I like working in small, closeknit teams. Sometimes workplace relationships are complex, focusing on doing the right thing always helps when conflicts happen and confrontation is necessary. Occassionally I like to hide in my bubble, after all I am an INTJ type designer, this just means I need space to re-energize and focus on things in front of me. But working with good people and having a good leader is what motivates me the most.

If you don't mind someone who is a bit out of the box but can pick up speed and solve your problems and don't mind training them up to be a good leader for your organization, I would love to speak with you as you are what I am looking for.
