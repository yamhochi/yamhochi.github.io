About Me

4 years ago I detoured from being a designer. It turned out to be the best thing that ever happened to me. As "some philosopher" notes, "some philosophy about perspective, you need to be away from the subject in order to see the subject clearer". Being away from design helped me experience design from a whole new perspective. I felt more compelled to critique things that were flawed about design. I found it easier to admit mistakes I've made as a designer. Perhaps that was it, it's about being able to swallow my pride and move on with life.

The industry that I was in does not use visuals as a primary expression, language and communication was vital to getting anything done, at all. If only I had knew this earlier. As designers, we are often camouflaged by our visual craft and perfected products. Designers tend to leave it there thinking that the job is done. The truth is, we seldom have control over what happens beyond that. Designers deserve to make critical decisions just as much as any company executive does. But in order to get there, and we need to improve the way we speak, write and communicate with businesses if we want to get anything done, at all.

The biggest lesson I learned from this was I've found purpose and motivationio in going really hard for what I want. My design philosophy is no different from any blog, article, book that has been written before. What differentiates me is I have what it takes to get things done and make things happen.

If you don't mind someone who is a bit out of the box but can pick up speed and good at solving problems, I think we'll work really well together.

If you don't mind training them up to be a good leader for your organization, I would love to speak with you too.

If you just want someone to listen to your blue sky thinkerings, drop me a line, I am always up for a good chat :)
