My slightly unusual encounters with design

Four years ago, ventured beyond the design world to search for ‘answers’ about design. I wanted to try out different things, I wanted to learn to create designs that go beyond the surface.

I stumbled upon an unusual combination of user experience, consulting and urban planning. I designed an app that improves wayfinding for visually impaired people, I built econometric models that helped government find the best locations for investment, I came up with a solution to make reading urban planning reports easier. I combined these expertise and developed a platform to help raise awareness about the environment.

I realised, design can do many things beyond our imagination. It bridges the gap between people and technology, it turns abstract thoughts into tangible solutions. It balances the mystery between art and science. All these can happen, only if we took initiative and helped others envision what a better version of the future might look like.

If you’re looking for someone who is passionate about people and technology, who can turn ideas into visuals, who can design with code, who can bridge the gap between your business and your users, lets have a chat about what we can do together.
