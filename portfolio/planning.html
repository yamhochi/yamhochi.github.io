Smart tools, smarter cities

April 2016

Proof of concept User analytics Hypotesis testing

Complex, rapidly updated data now comes from everywhere as organisations move their services onto digital platforms and we move closer to the ‘internet of things’ and the ‘smart city’.

This is my personal journey where I've removed the barrier between urban planning and technology in an organisation.

The challenge

In consulting, project findings are often condensed into long and wordy reports. They offer little interaction to cients, and results were difficult to understand. I proposed to the business that they should be using platform-based reports to improve client engagement and interaction.

Solutions

Hypotheses and Proof of Concept

I researched a number of solutions and platforms that can be easily integrated with our current website. I developed prototypes and widgets for blogs and bulletins as proof of concept for this idea.

Analytics

I monitored the site's page views and browsing time to gauge users' interest in the product. Whenever there was a new blog post, I would release new features and functions based user engagement metrics and feedback from colleagues.

I also came up with a system to estimate the profitability of the product. I developed plans to help the business set long term and short term goals for this prototype. I also taught junior colleagues how to code and facilitated workshops to discuss opportunities in this area.

Results

I managed to build up a portfolio of clients in a short amount of time and persuaded the firm to further invest in this area. I lead the 'digital solutions' team to completing their first official assignment with a major NSW Government client and leveraged the firm's image as a key innovator in the area.
