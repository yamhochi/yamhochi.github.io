Sharing and Enabling Environmental Data

August 2016

User journeys Information architecture Spatial search

SEED is a data plaform for everyone in the community, including landowners, researchers, local government, industry and special interest groups. Because of the vastness of information behind it, there was a strong focus on visualization, discoverability in order to help the community find what they needed.

The challenge

"Map Search" was an important feature of the site seeing there were many resources with geolocation attributes. Users requested an interface that imitated Google's place search workflow. But due to platform constraints, the final product was not as intuitive as anticipated.

I designed a solution to work around the technical constraints. I also persuaded the business to invest more resources on this storey due to its complexity and overarching importance to the platform.

Solutions

Putting pen to paper

Users were asked to provide feedback on the prototype and draw out what was expected from the map search interface. User journey maps, sketches and wireframes were used throughout the process to help my team better understand users' mental model and map out their thinking process.

Information Architecture

The major problem was that users were performing 'search' from different databases at different parts of the site. I reviewed the information architecture and implemented a solution that streamlined and consolidated the overall Index for Search. This gave the team more flexibility to query and construct results and present them in a way that made sense to the users.

Peer review and validation

Working in pairs, we made sure that decisions were sense-checked and validated before transforming them into code. This built stronger alliances between teams and we were able to deliver an improved solution ahead of schedule.

Results

Users found the new interface helped achieve their goals much faster. This dramatically improved the number of queries performed, lowered the number of abandoned sessions, and the testing team gave their stamp of approval. Due to NDA reasons, detailed product shots will be released once the site is approved by the reviewing committee (est. October 2016).
