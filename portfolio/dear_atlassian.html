My story about growth and why I'm a good fit for Atlassian

Dear Josh,

4 years ago, I took a break from design. Not knowing what to do next, I stumbled upon urban planning. It was then, I developed my greatest strength as a UX designer. The challenges that I faced had little to do with design, or urban planning. Instead it was about helping the business realise the problem in hand and guiding them towards a better solution.

In that role, I implemented a digital solution that transformed the company's reporting system onto a digital platform. I did it because I saw how hard it was for clients to interrogate planning statistics on a PDF. I wanted to make reading reports easier for clients, but it was hard getting the company to see value in developing something like this.

I put in a lot of thought, demonstrating to the business that digital transformation was necessary. I built prototypes of all levels of fidelity to show to my colleagues what a better version of reporting might look like. I used metrics and results to convince the board that clients are more engaged when interacting with real time results. I built trust, keeping clients’ and colleagues informed about my progress and ensured their priorities were taken care of.

Finally, the product took off and clients started requesting for more ’digital’ products. I won my first major contract and proved that my hypothesis on point. The board saw potential in it and was willing to invest more resources towards digital solutions.

I realised design is as much about the strategy as it is about the craft. Solutions that were clear to me were not obvious to others because people had different perceptions about the problem. And as a designer, it is my responsibility to get people’s visions aligned so that we can work towards a common goal effectively. There goes a saying,

Design is helping others see what a better version of the world would look like.

This is the kind of designer I strive to be, and I believe, a quality that Atlassians seeks.

When I left SGS Economics and Planning, my manager thanked me for making their lives easier with the solutions that I brought in. My colleagues called me a star for repeatedly pulling off challenges that seemed almost impossible at first. I am extremely appreciative of the experience because I know it has helped me mature as a designer and as a leader.

This said, I know Atlassian as a company who values employees’ self-motivation in shaping things to the way they see it should be- to " be the change you seek ". I still remember walking into the old Atlassian office once when I saw a poster on the wall saying — “ Don’t #@!% the customer ”. It was a really strong statement and it never left my memory. Something about those sayings tells me I share your values about work and play, and I’d be a really good fit at Atlassian.

If you're looking for a designer who can quickly iterate - build, measure and ship ideas, who can be the middleman for developers, the business and customers, I might just be the person you need. Please let me know if we can have a chat about potential positions within the design team, I'm particularly interested in the growth team but I’m also open to other divisions as well. My contact details and CV can be found on the top right link of this page. Drop me a line so we can get the conversation started. Looking forward to hearing your thoughts.

Sincerely,
Andie
